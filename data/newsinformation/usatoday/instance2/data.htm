                    UMass coach Derek Kellogg supports Derrick Gordon coming out

                    Scott Gleeson, USA TODAY Sports 1:21 p.m. EDT April 9, 2014

                    Massachusetts Minutemen coach Derek Kellogg yells out against the Clemson Tigers during the first half at TD Arena.(Photo: Jeremy Brevard, USA TODAY Sports)

                    SHARE 139 CONNECT 47 TWEET 28 COMMENTEMAILMORE

                    UMass coach Derek Kellogg said sophomore guard Derrick Gordon was noticeably nervous when he told him he was gay over the phone last Sunday.

                    After Gordon delivered the news to his coach, Kellogg's response was quick and to the point.

                    RELATED: Gordon comes out as gay

                    "I told him we loved him, and he's part of our family no matter what," Kellogg told USA TODAY Sports by phone Wednesday morning. "I said, 'We'll be here for you through everything.' "

                    ***

                    Gordon became the first openly gay player in Division I men's college basketball on Wednesday, coming out to both ESPN and Outsports a week after sharing his story with his teammates.​

                    "I'm proud of him for coming out, I think it's really courageous what he's doing," Kellogg said. "He's talking about saving people's lives. Who doesn't want a kid like that on their team?"

                    Kellogg said he doesn't expect Gordon's sexual orientation to be an issue with his teammates in the locker room.

                    "I think this kind of thing can unite us more than anything," Kellogg said. "And now it's out in the open so I think that helps make it more comfortable. ... He wanted to finally be himself. He didn't want to hide anymore. We support him 100%."

                    Scott Gleeson, a national college basketball writer/digital producer for USA TODAY Sports, is on Twitter @ScottMGleeson.
