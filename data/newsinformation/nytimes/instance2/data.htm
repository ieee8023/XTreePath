    Charlotte Police Officer Indicted in Shooting

    By KIM SEVERSONJAN. 27, 2014

    Inside
      Advertising

      CHARLOTTE, N.C. — A grand jury on Monday indicted a white police officer who fired 10 shots into a young black man who had driven off the road in a suburban neighborhood here and was apparently looking for help.

      It was the second time a grand jury had heard the case. The first panel, which convened a week ago, did not indict the officer, Randall Kerrick, on voluntary manslaughter charges, but suggested that prosecutors come back with a lesser charge.

      Instead, prosecutors presented the same charge to a different group of jurors on Monday. This group indicted Mr. Kerrick on voluntary manslaughter charges in the death of Jonathan Ferrell, 24, a former star football player at Florida A&M who had been dropping off a friend early one morning in September when his car went off the road.

      He stumbled up an embankment and knocked on a door. A woman inside, home alone with her infant, called the police in a panic.

      Three officers arrived and saw Mr. Ferrell on a road leading to the public pool in a suburban development about 15 miles from downtown Charlotte. Mr. Kerrick fired 12 shots as Mr. Ferrell moved toward the officers.

      Mr. Kerrick joined the city’s animal control division in 2010 as a path to a police job. He was suspended and charged with voluntary manslaughter less than 24 hours after the shooting. The charges were brought by the North Carolina attorney general’s office.

      The case has underscored continuing concerns about oversight of the Charlotte-Mecklenburg Police Department. Mr. Ferrell’s brother and mother, who traveled from Tallahassee, Fla., to await the verdict, have filed a wrongful death suit, claiming that the department used excessive force and that race was a factor in the shooting.

      Mr. Ferrell’s mother, Georgia Ferrell, said one thought came into her head when she heard that the grand jury had indicted Mr. Kerrick: “God’s will must be done.”
