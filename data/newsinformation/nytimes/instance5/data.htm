    Florida Congressman, Facing Pressure After Cocaine Charge, Resigns

    By ASHLEY PARKERJAN. 27, 2014

    Inside

      WASHINGTON — Representative Trey Radel, Republican of Florida, will resign from Congress effective Monday evening, according to a letter he sent to House Speaker John A. Boehner of Ohio.

      Mr. Radel, 37, a freshman legislator, pleaded guilty in November to a misdemeanor charge of buying 3.5 grams of cocaine in Washington and was sentenced to a year of probation. Though he had quietly returned to Congress in January after nearly a month at a private rehabilitation center in Naples, Fla., Mr. Radel announced his resignation plans on Monday morning.

      Referring to his “personal struggles,” Mr. Radel wrote: “While I have dealt with those issues on a personal level, it is my belief that professionally I cannot fully and effectively serve as a United States representative to the place I love and call home, Southwest Florida.”

      When he was first charged, Mr. Radel said he struggled “with the disease of alcoholism.”

      Mr. Radel’s resignation was first reported by Politico.

      In his letter, Mr. Radel thanked his colleagues for their “tremendous support and encouragement.”

      “As an eternal optimist, I know there are great things in store for our country when we find ways to work together,” he wrote. “Whether it is as a father, a husband, or in any future endeavor, I hope to contribute what I can to better our country in the years to come.”

      Mr. Radel, a former television anchorman and conservative talk show host, represents Florida’s 19th Congressional District, which includes Naples and Fort Myers. He won election in 2012 with 62 percent of the vote.

      The district is solidly Republican, and his resignation is unlikely to benefit Democrats. But while Mr. Radel was still in rehabilitation, a growing number of prominent Republicans — including the chairman of the Florida Republican Party and Florida’s governor, Rick Scott — called for his resignation.

      Governor Scott, working with the office of Florida’s secretary of state, will set the date for a special election.

      Former Representative Connie Mack IV, who in 2012 gave up the 19th District seat for an unsuccessful Senate run, has been trying to rally support for another bid. Lizbeth Benacquisto, the majority leader of the Florida State Senate, is also considered a likely candidate.

      Paige Kreegel, a former state representative who finished third to Mr. Radel during the state’s Republican primary in 2012, had already declared a primary challenge to Mr. Radel, and on Monday commended him for his decision to step down.

      “I know the last several months have been trying for Trey and his family,” Mr. Kreegel said in a statement. “But today, he did the right thing for the people he represents and provided all of us a chance to move forward without the distractions of the past. The people of Southwest Florida deserve principled, conservative leadership.”
